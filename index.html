Methodology & Data Sources

This analysis integrates authoritative datasets from USGS, COTREX, CO-WRA, and NOAA using a weighted overlay algorithm to generate a composite risk index. Four key factors—terrain (30%), wildfire potential (25%), remoteness (25%), and weather exposure (20%)—are combined at 30-meter resolution to classify backcountry areas into four risk levels, supporting evidence-based recreation planning.

Data Sources

  • USGS 3DEP: High-resolution elevation data
  • COTREX: Colorado trail network (40,000 miles)
  • CO-WRA: Wildfire risk assessment
  • NOAA: Weather and climate data

Risk Factors

  • Terrain (30%): Slope, elevation, aspect
  • Wildfire (25%): Fire hazard potential
  • Remoteness (25%): Distance from help
  • Weather (20%): Exposure risk

Analysis Methods

  • Weighted Overlay: Multi-criteria evaluation
  • Spatial Resolution: 30-meter analysis grid
  • Classification: 4-level risk zones
  • Validation: Literature-based methodology

Academic Context

  • Course: GEOG 578 - Advanced GIS
  • Institution: NMSU
  • Framework: Carver et al. (2013) methodology
  • Application: Recreation safety planning
