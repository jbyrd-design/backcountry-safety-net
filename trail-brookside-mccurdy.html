Trail Description

The Brookside-McCurdy Trail is the longest maintained trail in Lost Creek Wilderness, offering a challenging full-day hike or excellent overnight backpacking opportunity. The trail traverses varied terrain from creek valleys to high ridgelines, showcasing the wilderness's diverse ecosystems.

Trail Segments:

  • Brookside Section (0-6.5 mi): Follows Brookside Creek through aspen and pine forests
  • Mile 3.2: Major creek crossing (bridge washed out 2019; ford required)
  • Mile 6.5: Junction with McCurdy Mountain Trail - elevation 9,800 ft
  • McCurdy Section (6.5-12.3 mi): Climbs to ridgeline with Front Range views
  • Mile 9.8: High point at 10,780 ft - exposed ridge
  • Mile 12.3: Terminus at McCurdy Park (common camping area)

Backpacking: Established campsites at miles 5.2, 7.8, and 11.5; water treatment required
